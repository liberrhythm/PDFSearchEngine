Evidence of the properties of an ambiguity tolerance measure: The Multiple Stimulus Types Ambiguity Tolerance Scale-II (MSTAT-II) 1

Article in Psychological Reports 105(3 Pt 1):975-88 · December 2009
DOI: 10.2466/PR0.105.3.975-988 · Source: PubMed
  • 1st David L. Mclain
    15.38 · State University of New York at Oswego
Abstract
Despite widespread interest in ambiguity tolerance and other information-related individual differences, existing measures of ambiguity tolerance are conceptually disparate and are often psychometrically weak. This paper presents evidence of reliability and validity for a 13-item measure of ambiguity tolerance (MSTAT-II) based on a definition of ambiguity tolerance as an orientation, ranging from aversion to attraction, toward stimuli that are complex, unfamiliar, and insoluble.
